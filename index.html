A.P.J Abdul kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam , popularly known as the "Missile Man of India," was one of the most respected and inspiring figures in Indian history. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he came from a humble background and rose to become a renowned scientist and the 11th President of India. Kalam played a crucial role in India's space and missile development programs, especially through his work with the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He was instrumental in the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles, which earned him national acclaim. As President from 2002 to 2007, he was widely loved for his humility, integrity, and dedication to the nation. Beyond his scientific achievements, Dr. Kalam was also a prolific writer and a passionate educator who encouraged young people to dream big and work hard to achieve their goals. He believed strongly in the power of youth and education to transform the future of a nation. His life remains a shining example of how determination, hard work, and vision can lead to greatness. Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, but his legacy continues to inspire millions across the world.
